Strategy Overview

Any car in Korea.
To your door. With the real numbers.

You're not competing on cars — everyone has the same Encar inventory. You're competing on a secret most of Europe and the Gulf has never heard, and on trust. Content manufactures both.

"Become the trusted young face of car exports from Korea — from a $6,000 Kia to a Rolls-Royce — by proving Korea is the most underrated used-car market on Earth."

The 5 knowledge bombs (your unfair advantage)

Left-hand drive

Unlike Japan or the UK — no steering conversion. Street-legal in Europe, the Balkans and the GCC as is. One sentence, infinite videos.

German luxury depreciates hard

Korea is a huge Mercedes/BMW market with fast trade-up culture. A 3–4-year-old S-Class in Seoul can undercut Stuttgart dramatically.

Radical transparency

Encar history reports show accidents, repairs, owners, insurance payouts — public and verifiable. Compare that to a Balkan used-car lot.

Condition culture

Garage-kept, dealer-serviced, low-mileage cars are the norm. Plus full spectrum in one market: Kia → Genesis → BMW → Porsche → Rolls-Royce.

Rules that protect the brand

  • Never crosspost with watermarks — re-export clean every time (IG penalizes recycled content).
  • Never buy followers or views — it poisons TikTok's test audience.
  • Real listings, real math, destination-correct taxes. One exposed fake number kills the brand.
  • No client personal data, plates in active deals, or payment details on camera.
  • Street supercar filming is fine; inside showrooms — ask first and offer the footage. Access is a long-term asset.

Toolkit

Hooks Library

A hook is three things at once: the first words, the first frame, and the first sound. Never start a video without choosing the hook first — script backwards from it. Fill blanks with real models, cities, numbers.

Toolkit

Visual Hooks — Background Bank

70 background elements for the first 3 seconds of any video — the Incongruity Overlay technique. Something rare, funny, or chaotic happens in frame while you deliver your normal car-export script. Never look at it, never mention it, never react — the gap between "calm business talk" and the background IS the hook. Zero new scripts needed.

Rule: rotate categories, don't repeat the same background twice in a row. Always ask permission for animals, fire, water, or other people's businesses — and never compromise your own safety for a shot.
Toolkit

Higgsfield Playbook

Rule #1: AI amplifies your real footage — it never replaces it. This matters double in the luxury tier: a Gulf buyer considering a six-figure car forgives zero fakeness.

One-time setup

  1. Free tier to test; upgrade once you know which workflows you use weekly. Test on images before burning video credits.
  2. Soul ID: upload 10–20 clean photos of your face (varied angles, good light) → persistent AI character of you.
  3. Asset folders: /RAW (batch footage by date) · /HERO-FRAMES (AI inputs) · /HF-OUTPUT · /SOUNDS · /READY (clean 9:16 exports).
  4. Test 3 Cinema Studio presets on one real car frame before using any in a post.

Core workflow — image-to-video from YOUR frames

Shoot deliberate hero frames on batch day (¾ front low angle, dead-on grille, badge, interior POV) → upload to Higgsfield → apply camera preset → generate 3–5s clip → sandwich it between real shots. Cut that way, it reads as expensive cinematography, not AI.

Signature recipes

A · Car of the Week (15s, weekly franchise)

Sound first → real walkaround 3s → details 2×2s → Higgsfield 360 orbit on the drop 3s → rear shot 2s → price card + handle. Alternate value weeks (Genesis, Tucson) with luxury weeks (911, G-Wagon, Ghost).

B · Price-reveal beat edit (12–14s)

Build-up with "TIRANA DEALER PRICE: €19,000" text → crash-zoom on the drop + real Seoul price slams on → landed math in buyer's currency + "comment your city."

B2 · Gangnam luxury edit (12–15s, GCC)

Real street-spotting footage. Pace it SLOW: 2 real shots (3s each) → slow dolly-in on grille 3s → neon wide 3s → "Seoul. DM." card. Calm sound. The GCC audience stops on confidence, not chaos.

C · The impossible transition (10–12s)

Zoom IN on car A's grille + zoom OUT from car B's = seamless teleport cut at max zoom. Comments ask how → answer with a video reply (free second video).

D · Soul ID (1–2×/month max)

Place yourself in scenes you can't shoot — stylized-obviously, framed as creative flex. Never fake inspections or deals with it.

E · URL-to-video (Marketing Studio)

Listing URL → formatted ad cutdowns. Too ad-shaped for organic feed — save for paid retargeting, month 3+.

Model selection

  • Kling 2.6 → product close-ups, detail motion (best surface/reflection fidelity on cars)
  • Sora 2 → hero/establishing shots, complex scenes
  • Veo 3.1 → fast social cutdowns, good short-clip coherence
  • Compare once on the same hero frame, then standardize. Credit budget: ~10–15 generations/week max.

What NOT to do

  • No fully-AI cars presented as real inventory — a generated Rolls passed off as a listing = brand death in the only tier where one deal is worth $10K+.
  • No AI voiceovers — your real voice and accent ARE the brand.
  • No Higgsfield watermark in posts — export clean.
  • No AI luxury-fantasy slop (fake Lambos in fake Dubai) — most saturated category of 2026. Your moat is the opposite: real supercars on real Seoul streets with real prices. You film what others can only generate.
